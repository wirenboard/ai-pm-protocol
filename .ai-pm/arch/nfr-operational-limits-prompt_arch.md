# nfr-operational-limits-prompt — design notes

## Context

EPIC whole-system-property auditor, slice 3 (slice 1 = `invariants-index`, v2.21.0; slice 2 = `taxonomy-drift-sweep`, v2.22.0). The downstream review found that a resource-constrained / scale-bearing project writes its resource and scale budget **nowhere** — not a discipline lapse but a **missing prompt**: the protocol never forces "what is this feature's / the system's resource and scale budget?". This slice adds a **conditional NFR / operational-limits check** to `/pm-plan` (fires on a scale-bearing feature OR a resource-constrained platform) and gives NFRs a **home split by audience**: user-facing limits → the Product Contract `## Must not break`; resource budgets → a NEW conditional `## Operational limits & budgets` section in `docs/architecture.md`.

Prose-spec / meta-feature on the template repo (software-kind, no-user-facing-contract exception — every scenario subject is `/pm-plan` / the planner / `pm-architect` / a template, non-human). No runtime, no shared state. "Adjacent implementations" are the protocol's own established patterns — the conditional pre-draft checks, the conditional-architecture-section discipline, and the soft-enforced no-hook family — which I map as such.

The genuine forks are narrow. Per the brief, I **settle by sibling precedent** everything the slices before already fixed (the audience-split homes, the `N/A`/`[?]`-not-invented discipline, the post-coding handoff, proportionality) and concentrate the variant work on the one real structural fork (Q1: a new section vs folding into `## Architectural constraints`) plus two judgement calls the brief raises (Q2 detection signal, Q3 no-hard-gate altitude).

## Adjacent patterns (protocol patterns of the same shape)

1. **The conditional pre-draft checks in `/pm-plan`** — `pm-plan.md:81` (Stack-component), `:104` (Interaction-scenario), `:119` (Security-surface). All four are "mandatory, no PM questions" / judgment-triggered, silent when their trigger is absent, recording into existing-owner homes — never a new mandatory section. The Security-surface check (`:119–126`) is the closest twin: it keys on a **presence signal** (`docs/threat-model.md` present ⇒ security-bearing), is silent on a non-security project, and rides the **same post-coding "Docs to update" handoff** to `pm-architect`. The new NFR check sits beside these as a same-shaped conditional and (for the user-facing half) reuses the existing **product-vs-technical question rule** verbatim.

2. **The conditional-architecture-section discipline** — `architecture.md.tmpl:58` (`## Integration contract`), `:64` (`## Behavioral contract`), `:81` (`### System invariants`). Every conditional section carries `N/A — <reason>` when empty, is `[?]` when unknown, and is **never invented to fill** (`pm-architect.md` A2, `:62`). The `## Behavioral contract` is the canonical model: born `N/A`, walked by A2, excluded from the A4 cross-check set (`:66`) because it is authored domain content with no external artifact to diverge from. The new `## Operational limits & budgets` section is a direct sibling of this shape.

3. **The Product Contract `## Must not break` home** — `contract.md.tmpl:29`. A quantified user-facing limit (max devices/endpoints, perceived throughput) is a **promise to the user**, so it is a Must-not-break item by nature; the slice adds one note line, no structural change. The PM-language / token-free discipline already governing that section (`:48`) applies.

4. **The soft-enforced + no-hook family** — `doc/architecture.md:138` (threat-model lifecycle), `:154`/`:277` (product-readiness gate), `:166` (doc-migration registry), `:208` (automode). A consistent, repeatedly-affirmed protocol stance: when the trigger is a **semantic judgement a regex cannot make**, **no `PreToolUse` hook** is added; enforcement (if any) is prose at the gate plus presence-keyed backstops, never a hook that would mis-decide the trigger. The NFR fire-condition ("scale-bearing? resource-constrained?") is exactly such a judgement — so this slice's no-hook choice is family-consistent, and its no-*gate* choice (Q3) is the question of whether even a backstop earns its keep.

## Behavioral risks in this area

Not event-driven code — no subscription/feedback surface. The risks here are **protocol-design** risks: (a) a new architecture section that does not earn its keep bloats the A2 walk-list and the `N/A`-noise every project carries (Q1); (b) a detection signal that over- or under-fires either nags every tiny project or misses a real budget (Q2); (c) a hard gate on a judgement-triggered prompt that violates proportionality, or conversely no backstop at all letting a misjudged trigger drop a budget silently (Q3). The whole slice's value is durability of a *recorded* budget; the risks are all about not paying for that durability with noise or a false wall.

## Settling the questions

### Q2 — the detection signal (settled: irreducible judgement, soundest formulation named)

The brief asks whether there is a durable **presence-signal** analogous to "`threat-model.md` present ⇒ security-bearing". **There is not, and one must not be manufactured.** The threat-model signal works because security-bearing-ness is a *durable property of the project* with a *dedicated artifact* whose existence is itself the bootstrap-time decision. "Scale-bearing feature" is a property of **this feature** (does its core scale with a count the system must bound — devices / endpoints / connections / sessions / messages-per-second / queue depth?), re-judged every plan; there is no per-feature artifact to key on. "Resource-constrained platform" is closer to a durable property, but its only honest signals are **read, not present**: an embedded / limited-RAM / Docker-on-controller note in `docs/stack-notes.md`, or an "embedded / offline / must-fit-X" boundary in `docs/architecture.md` `## Architectural constraints`. Those are content to *read and judge*, not a file whose mere presence settles it — manufacturing a marker file (a `.resource-constrained` flag) would be a new required artifact, contradicting the slice's "no new mandatory artifact" remark and the no-hook family.

**Soundest formulation:** the trigger is an **OR of two judgements**, mirroring the Security-surface check's read-and-judge shape but with **no presence shortcut**:
- *scale-bearing feature* — judged from the feature description against the bounded-count list, re-judged each plan;
- *resource-constrained platform* — judged from `docs/stack-notes.md` / `docs/architecture.md` `## Architectural constraints`, a (mostly) durable read.

This is correct because it is **irreducibly a judgement** (the plan's scenario 3 says so: "a semantic judgement a regex cannot make — the same no-hook reality as the product-readiness gate"). Naming it as judgement, not presence, is the soundness point.

**Over/under-fire risk for the PM (named, not a defect):**
- *Under-fire (the accepted residual):* the orchestrator misjudges a feature as non-scale, or misses a constraint line, and no prompt fires → the budget goes unwritten. This is the silent-degradation the plan accepts (Q3) and the homes make recoverable at the next refresh.
- *Over-fire:* the orchestrator treats an ordinary feature on an ordinary platform as scale-bearing and surfaces a budget question that produces only `N/A`. Low-cost (one silent-resolving question, no PM nag on the technical half) but worth keeping the bounded-count list **concrete** in the spec so "scale-bearing" is not read as "any feature that touches more than one thing".

Settling note: gate the *resource-constrained* read on the **two named sources only** (`stack-notes.md`, `## Architectural constraints`) so the signal stays a documented read, not free association — the same discipline the Security-surface check applies by pointing at the single-source surface list.

### Q3 — the no-hard-gate decision (settled: right altitude this slice; a did-it-right check is a *later-slice* candidate, not this one)

The plan deliberately omits both a `pm-plan-checker` DoD block and a `pm-auditor` presence-note. **This is the right altitude for this slice**, for a reason stronger than proportionality alone: a *did-it-at-all* gate is **structurally impossible** here. A DoD block "did the NFR check fire when it should have?" requires the gate to **re-derive the semantic trigger** the orchestrator already judged — the gate would have to itself decide "is this feature scale-bearing?", which is the exact judgement no regex/checker can make. That is why the Security-surface DoD block can exist (it gates on a *presence* signal — threat-model.md present AND a named surface touched, both greppable) and an NFR did-it-at-all block cannot (its trigger is pure judgement). Pulling a did-it-at-all gate into this slice would not be over-engineering; it would be **incoherent**, and would re-introduce the false-block-on-substrate-work failure the no-hook family exists to avoid. The plan's recorded trade-off — the prompt **degrades silently** if the orchestrator misjudges, acceptable because the homes make a *recorded* budget durable and auditable — is the correct framing.

The brief's sharper question: is a **narrow "if NFRs were declared, are they in the right home?" did-it-*right* check** (not a did-it-at-all gate) worth pulling into THIS slice? Such a check **is** coherent — it is presence-keyed and shape-not-meaning: *if* a `## Operational limits & budgets` section is non-`N/A`, do its entries look like resource budgets (not user-facing limits); *if* a contract `## Must not break` carries a quantified-limit item, is it user-facing — i.e. a home-correctness check, the direct sibling of the threat↔constraint dangling-`SCn` check (`pm-auditor.md:140` family) and the slice-2 journeys drift sweep. It is the *kind* of thing this EPIC adds. **But it should not land in this slice**, for two reasons:

1. **No corpus to check yet.** The homes do not exist until this slice ships and downstream projects start recording into them. A did-it-right auditor check written now would have zero non-`N/A` inputs to validate against and would be tuned against the template's own (empty) section — the same premature-tuning trap slice 2's Q3 flagged for the `active`/`done` example enum. Better to ship the homes, see real budgets land, then scope the check against real shapes.
2. **The EPIC is explicitly sliced this way.** Slice 1 added the home (index), slice 1's *auditor* check and slice 2 followed. The home-first / check-next cadence is the EPIC's established rhythm (`doc/architecture.md:216`). A did-it-right check is the natural **slice-4 candidate** ("if NFRs were declared, are they in the right home?"), recorded in Out of scope (plan line 72) exactly as a future narrow check — keep it there.

**Recommendation on Q3:** keep this slice gate-free as planned; record the did-it-right check as a named future slice in the decision record (the plan already does, line 72). Altitude is correct.

### Settled-by-precedent (no variant work needed)

- **Audience-split homes (sc4/sc5):** user-facing → contract `## Must not break`; resource → the new architecture section. Settled — this is the contract-two-layer / one-fact-one-owner split the protocol uses everywhere (threat=risk vs constraint=rule; user value vs machine taxonomy). No re-litigation.
- **`N/A`-default / `[?]`-not-invented / post-coding handoff / A2 walk-list add:** all inherited verbatim from the `## Behavioral contract` precedent and the threat-model handoff. The new section is born `N/A`, walked by A2, authored on the "Docs to update" handoff. **A4 note:** the new section, like the Behavioral contract, has **no external artifact to cross-check against** (a RAM ceiling is authored engineering content, not derivable from the tree/CI/README) — so it must **not** enter the A4 cross-check set, which stays exactly three pairings. Call this out for the coder so the section is added to A2 (walk) but not A4 (cross-check), mirroring the Behavioral-contract exclusion at `pm-architect.md:66`.
- **No hook:** family-consistent (see Adjacent pattern 4); not re-argued.

## The real fork — Q1: new `## Operational limits & budgets` section vs fold into `## Architectural constraints`

### Variant A: new conditional `## Operational limits & budgets` section (plan's choice — recommended)

- **Where:** a new conditional `##` section in `architecture.md.tmpl`, placed **beside `## Architectural constraints`** (its sibling hard-boundary), `N/A — <reason>` default; added to `pm-architect` A2 walk-list and Section A ownership/handoff.
- **Relation to adjacent:** symmetric with `## Behavioral contract` / `## Integration contract` — a conditional, born-`N/A`, A2-walked, A4-excluded, authored-on-handoff section. A *quantified budget* is a distinct content shape (a number + unit + scope: "RAM ≤ 64 MB", "boot ≤ 8 s", "≤ 200 endpoints") with a distinct audience (an engineer sizing the platform), warranting its own labelled home the way taxonomies earned theirs.
- **Pros:** a budget gets a **named, greppable home** the NFR prompt can reference by section name (sc5 requires "reference the new section by name, no re-encoding" — a named section makes that pointer real); a future did-it-right check (Q3 slice-4) keys on section presence + shape, which needs the section to *be* a section; quantified budgets do not dilute the qualitative-boundary list in `## Architectural constraints` (which reads as rules-not-to-violate, not as sized numbers); inherits the `N/A`/`[?]` discipline cleanly.
- **Cons:** one more section on the A2 walk-list and one more `N/A` line every non-constrained project carries — the genuine cost the brief flags. Mild: it is exactly the cost `## Behavioral contract` and `## Integration contract` already pay, and the section is born `N/A` so the walk is one line.
- **Risks:** none structural. The only discipline to keep exact: it must be **A2-walked but A4-excluded** (see Settled-by-precedent), and `[?]`-not-invented for un-quantifiable budgets (a number the orchestrator cannot know without real platform knowledge is `[?]`, never a guessed ceiling).

### Variant B: fold resource budgets into the existing `## Architectural constraints`

- **Where:** no new section; a quantified budget is written as another bullet in `## Architectural constraints` ("RAM must stay ≤ 64 MB — reason: embedded deployment"), which already hosts the "must work offline / embedded deployment" boundary the resource-constrained signal reads from.
- **Relation to adjacent:** plausible — a budget *is* a thing-that-must-not-be-violated-without-PM-decision, the literal definition of `## Architectural constraints` (`tmpl:42`). The resource-constrained signal is *already read from this section*, so the budget would live where its own trigger lives.
- **Pros:** zero new section, zero A2 walk-list growth, zero `N/A`-noise for projects that have constraints but no quantified budget; keeps all hard boundaries in one list.
- **Cons:** (1) **no clean pointer target** — sc5's "route resource budgets → \<named home\> and reference it by name" degrades to "add a bullet to `## Architectural constraints`", and a future did-it-right check (Q3) cannot key on "is this bullet a resource budget vs a qualitative rule" without prose-policing the bullet list — exactly the shape-not-meaning line the auditor family refuses to cross. (2) **mixes two content shapes** — a quantified-number budget and a qualitative no-external-services rule read differently and are audited differently; co-locating them makes the budget harder to find and harder to check. (3) `## Architectural constraints` is **not conditional** (it has no `N/A`-default contract), so a born-`N/A` budget concept does not fit its existing shape — you would be bolting conditional-section semantics onto a non-conditional section.
- **Risks:** the budget hides as one bullet among qualitative rules and drifts un-auditable; the EPIC's home-then-check cadence (Q3) has no section to attach a future check to.

## Recommendation

**Variant A (new conditional `## Operational limits & budgets` section), as the plan proposes.** A quantified resource budget is a *distinct content shape with a distinct audience* — a sized engineering bound, not a qualitative don't-violate rule — and giving it a named, conditional, born-`N/A` home (the proven `## Behavioral contract` shape) buys three things folding into `## Architectural constraints` cannot: a real pointer target for the NFR prompt's "reference by name" requirement (sc5), a section a future did-it-right auditor check can key on by presence-and-shape (Q3 slice-4) without prose-policing a mixed bullet list, and clean inheritance of the `N/A`/`[?]`-not-invented discipline. The cost — one A2 walk-list line and one `N/A` per project — is the same modest cost the other conditional sections already pay and is the right price for an auditable, referenceable home. Keep it **A2-walked but A4-excluded** (authored engineering content, no external artifact — the Behavioral-contract exclusion).

## Risks for the PM

1. **Keep the section A2-walked but A4-excluded (the one thing to get exact in coding).** A RAM ceiling / boot budget is authored engineering content with no tree/CI/README artifact to diverge from — so the new section is added to `pm-architect`'s A2 walk-list (so it is walked and born `N/A`) but **must not** enter the A4 cross-check set (which stays exactly three pairings). This mirrors the `## Behavioral contract` exclusion (`pm-architect.md:66`); getting it wrong would manufacture a self-inflicted A4 finding on every authored budget.

2. **The trigger is irreducibly a judgement — name it as such, give it no presence shortcut (Q2).** There is no durable "file present ⇒ resource-constrained" signal analogous to threat-model.md; the resource-constrained read must be gated to the **two named sources** (`docs/stack-notes.md`, `docs/architecture.md` `## Architectural constraints`) and the scale-bearing judgement to the **concrete bounded-count list**, so "scale-bearing" is not read loosely as "any feature touching more than one thing". Accept the under-fire (a misjudged trigger drops the prompt silently) — the homes make a recorded budget recoverable at the next refresh.

3. **No hard gate this slice — and a did-it-*at-all* gate would be incoherent, not merely over-built (Q3).** A DoD/auditor "did the NFR check fire?" block would have to re-derive the semantic trigger no checker can evaluate — that is why it is omitted, consistent with the no-hook family. A narrow **did-it-right** check ("if NFRs were declared, are they in the right home?") *is* coherent and is the natural **slice-4** candidate, but should not land now: there is no non-`N/A` corpus yet to tune it against (the premature-tuning trap), and the EPIC's home-first/check-next cadence puts it in a later slice. Keep it recorded in Out of scope (plan line 72), not pulled forward.

4. **`[?]`-not-invented for budgets (inherited, but load-bearing here).** A budget the orchestrator cannot quantify without real platform knowledge is `[?]` for the PM to confirm — **never a guessed number**. A confabulated "RAM ≤ 64 MB" is worse than `[?]` because it reads as authoritative; the existing Behavioral-contract "never invent to fill" discipline applies verbatim and the coder must carry it into the new section's guidance line.
